Super Hyper Space

Super Hyper Space is a neo-classical top-down shooter, taking place in a futuristic space setting.
Destroy hordes of extraterrestrial spaceships and blast your way through the galaxy.

With Super Hyper Space I wanted to create a good balanced and simple shoot ‘em up game with an upgrade Mechanic that everybody can enjoy. My goal was to develop a game from start to finish by myself. This includes not only the game design but also the creation of graphics and programming. During the development I learned a lot about game develop and Player Feedback helped me to improve the game even further.

Upgrade Mechanic

The game has an upgrade mechanism. You can buy new weapons, improve your shield or buy addons that for example attract coins from further distance or show the life of the enemy. For this the player needs more energy provided by an energy core.

To Upgrade you need to collect coins, you get for destroying enemies. While stronger opponents lose more coins, stronger upgrades cost more coins.

While the main game loops is all about collect more coins to buy better upgrades to defeat stronger enemies. It's also about organizing the energy costs. For this purpose, players have the possibility to choose which weapons and upgrades they want to equip. On some missions, a faster ship with a weapon with a large radius is a good choice. On other missions, a better shield and a weapon with more penetrating power is an advantage.
The game gives each player the opportunity to play with his own style.

In order to organize the energy costs optimally, the game offers a preview window in which the player can see how much energy the current equipment consumes. Below that is a window in which the costs are listed in detail, so that the players have a good overview of all costs

Lost Legend

Lost Legend is a retro 2D action role-playing game with a fantasy-SciFi scenario. It combines the charming graphics style of the old SNES era with modern gameplay, a fast fighting system and tricky puzzles - and all this served up with a charming story.

Inspired by early 16 bit snes games like Secret of Mana, Legend of Zelda, Chrono Trigger and Final Fantasy 6.
As much a love these games, it was always my dream to make such a game with my own ideas. I really like to live out my creativity in different things. Is it creating graphics or designing puzzles, programming mechanics or even telling a story.

Level Design

Watching the web series Bosskeys of the Youtube channel GameMakers Toolkit. I learned a lot about non-linear level design in games like Zelda, Dark Souls, and Metroid. This helped me a lot in creating my level design. I took over the graph system from the creator Mark Brown for creating my own level graphs. Any smallkey is used to opening any locked door . Each key object can be used to overcome many obstacles . And every boss key can be used to open exactly one boss door . The example below shows a relatively simple layout for one level. But with the system more complex levels are possible, as you can see here for the game Super Metroid. I am currently working on an algorithm that randomly generates these graphs.
